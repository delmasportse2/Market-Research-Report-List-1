<p><p>Railway signalling relays are essential for controlling train movements and ensuring safety on the tracks. The market is divided into two main types: DC (direct current) and AC (alternating current) relays. DC relays typically operate on lower voltage and are commonly used in older systems, providing reliable performance in various conditions. In contrast, AC relays are favored for their ability to handle higher voltage levels and efficiency in modern signalling systems. Each type plays a crucial role in maintaining safe and efficient railway operations.</p></p>
